## 1. CHARACTERS (As Known to the Reader)

### VAELEN — Senior Practitioner, Pattern Reader
**Physical:** Tall — noticeably, has to angle shoulders through standard doorways. Long face, dark eyes, handsome in a landscape way (not pretty, not symmetrical, but arranged so you keep looking). Faint lines at corners of mouth. Shadows under eyes (fatigue or permanent). Past thirty. Energy reads younger — quick, magnetic, slightly restless.
**Behavior on page:** Eats Ashara smokebread one-handed through chaos. Arm slung over carriage side with tavern-chair looseness. Grin that transforms his face — softens angles, warms eyes, makes people feel like the only person in the room (supply clerk walked into a door frame). When reading the network: face empties, head tilts, mechanical precision — then snaps back seamlessly. Left hand closes into a fist when worried — the only part of him that doesn't perform ease.
**Competence shown:** Extracted 18 months of node data from a 3-second unfocused stare at 200 meters. Identified soil depletion pattern, Mirathyn overconsumption, downstream compensation. Read Drenna's frontier origin from her boots/posture/walk pattern. [Ch 2] Identified exact spot and timing of field failure by reading "scars" in the network — "fourteen months ago, this exact spot." At the node since first light, motionless for hours. Described his gift as "reading a scar — the tissue tells you when the wound happened, how deep it went, which direction the blade was moving." Said "the network is lying" — flat, pragmatic, a problem to solve.
**Relationships:** Leads the team through warmth, not hierarchy. Introduces people by contribution, not rank. Drops professional polish slightly around the team. Something unreadable flickers across his face when Sorvyn declares faith in him — gone in a half-second. [Ch 2] When Drenna shares personal information about her father, he doesn't say "I'm sorry" or deflect — he lets the silence sit, then asks to see her data from other communities. Treats community observation as data, not anecdote — first institutional practitioner Drenna has met who does this.
**What the reader suspects:** The warmth might be a performance. The grin arrives a half-beat late once. Something mechanical underneath. Genuinely gifted or very skilled at appearing so — Caeleth can't tell.
**What the reader now knows (Ch 3):** The warmth IS deliberate — he chooses it, produces it, deploys it. He's been doing it since age 16. But it's also genuine — both at once. His mind processes everything automatically ("the way light arrives through an open window"). He noticed Drenna left before dawn and returned with different soil on her boots. The deep reading costs him physically: headache behind left eye, metallic taste, hand tremors that are getting worse. He loves the Amarvel — raw, helpless, the one unguarded thing about him. He admitted to Drenna "I don't know how to do that" — the most honest thing he's said in months. He thinks: "Sorvyn trusts me completely and I am not at all certain I deserve it."
**What the reader doesn't know yet:** The full wound, the serial chase pattern, what broke him, his relationship history.

- **Veldryn:** Referenced in anecdote — ice shelves, crevasses. Cold climate.
- **Ashara:** Referenced via smokebread — volcanic region, volcanic salt.

### Food & Drink
- **Ashara smokebread:** Dark crust, flecked with volcanic salt that glints in the light. Carried as travel food.
- **Community stew:** Root vegetables and smoked grain. Hot, filling. Should taste like more than it does.
- **Community water:** Tastes like nothing. Not bad, not mineral. "Like drinking the memory of water." Started 4 weeks ago. People already used to it.
- **Community breakfast:** [Ch 2] Boiled grain, dried fruit, tea brewed from something local that tastes like warm earth.

### Creatures
- **Bond-runners:** Transport creatures. Different temperaments (Ylveth = 140 years old, calm grandmother; Korveth = 19, wild). Bonded to handlers, not passengers. Bond visible as lichen patterns on body.
- **Companion creatures (personal bonds):** [Ch 2] Smaller than runners. Drenna's father's creature — medium-sized, furred, broad-headed. Bond formed young (father bonded at 17). Bond persists even when person deteriorates. Creature waits indefinitely.

### Magic/Network
- **Network nodes:** Faint shimmer in the air above concentration points. Supposed to pulse gold-green with Amarwen's rhythm. This one's pulse is irregular.
- **Pattern reading:** Vaelen's ability. Eyes go distant, head tilts, face empties. Extracts data invisibly. Others can't interrupt him when he does it. [Ch 2] Described as "reading scars" — can identify exact location and timing of network failures. Can stand at a node motionless for hours.
- **Network stress shown:** Node compensating for downstream deficit. "Like a body pulling blood from extremities to protect the core." 18 months of gradual decline.
- **Lichen pulse:** Gold-green wave of light that ripples across bonded creature when handler touches it. [Ch 2] Also pulses faintly on companion creatures when bonded person moves — even when person is effectively gone.
- **Mirathyn fields:** [Ch 2] Living stalks that respond to energy — lean toward children running between rows. When the field declines, stalks stop responding even if output is technically within parameters. Children sense the wrongness before instruments do.
- **The gap:** [Ch 2] Vaelen's term. The disconnect between what the network reports (functional, within parameters) and ground reality (people disappearing, water tasteless, children withdrawing). "The network is lying" — not intentionally, but its self-diagnostic doesn't measure what's actually failing.

### Culture
- **Frontier threadwork:** Thin bands at the wrist. Local plant dyes, family patterns passed down. Marks frontier origin.
- **Solvarei field markings:** Worn on uniform. New ones are stiff with bright insignia.
- **Academy:** Trains practitioners. Instructor Halveth referenced — conducts assessments, teaches that appearing calm in panic-worthy conditions is the mark of a good practitioner.
- **Regional office/specialists:** Bureaucratic. Filed the node decline as "cyclical." Sent a node specialist last season who was wrong.
- **Solcarin (elite):** [Ch 2] Referenced — Drenna attended "the most prestigious" one. Different from the one Vaelen will attend. Students from privilege, no frontier representation. Drenna modulated her accent, learned to perform belonging. Patrons funded her. The institution lets people in but doesn't make room for them.

### Currency
- **Coins:** Silver, copper, half-marks. Half-mark bears face of a Hearthlands governor.
- **Deployment rates:** 1.4 marks per hour for runner transport.

## 5. ESTABLISHED RULES (Must Stay Consistent)

- **Network reading** looks like: eyes go distant, head tilts, face empties for 3-4 seconds. Nobody interrupts. Vaelen snaps back seamlessly. [Ch 2] Can also be sustained for hours at a node — standing motionless, eyes closed, hands at sides.
- **Bond creature communication:** Handler uses low guttural tone, more felt than heard. Not Rootsong — something physical/vibrational.
- **Lichen pulse:** Gold-green. Travels outward from point of contact across creature's body. Fades. [Ch 2] On companion creatures, pulses faintly with bonded person's movements even when person is effectively gone.
- **Node shimmer:** Gold-green. Pulses with Amarwen's rhythm. Visible in the air above concentration point. [Ch 2] Very faint nodes = barely visible even at midday.
- **Ethura signs in early stage:** Sensory muting (color fading, taste disappearing). Behavioral flattening (smiles that don't travel, movements too regular). People adapt to the wrongness and stop noticing it. The adaptation is more concerning than the symptom. [Ch 2] Temperature drops (colder than seasonal). Mirathyn stalks stop leaning toward children. The decline has a sequence — a grammar.
- **Mirathyn behavior:** [Ch 2] Living stalks respond to children's energy by leaning toward them. This responsiveness fails before output metrics show decline. Children detect the change before instruments.
- **Bond persistence vs. bond destruction:** [Ch 2] When the Amarvel thins naturally, bonds weaken but persist — lichen fades but remains. When concentrated Mirathyn extract is used, the bond can be BURNED THROUGH — lichen goes patchy and dark, dead in places, creature stops responding entirely. Different mechanisms, different damage. Natural weakening = recoverable. Chemical destruction = permanent.
- **Mirathyn extract spectrum:** [Ch 2] Low dose = pinch in tea, cultural, mostly harmless. Concentrated brew = frontier black market, unlicensed traders, escalating dependency. Destroys bonds rather than restoring them. The irony: people take it to feel the connection the weakened Amarvel took away, and it destroys the connection permanently.
- **The parallel:** [Ch 2] Mirathyn abuse and systemic Amarvel decline produce the SAME SIGNS — person going quiet, emptying, body continuing without the person. Different causes, identical symptoms. Why the Amarvel produces the same kind of absence regardless of cause is an open question the characters haven't answered yet.
- **Vaelen's scar-reading:** [Ch 2] He describes network failures as scars — the tissue tells you when the wound happened, how deep, which direction the blade moved. Can pinpoint exact location and timing of failure onset.
- **Pattern-reading full sensory experience (Ch 3):** The Amarvel opens like a conversation. Three layers: trunk bass (deep, slow, Amarwen's own rhythm), branch midrange (complex, carrying nutrient/information traffic), capillary chatter (high, fast, anxious at the edges). Old Amarvel vibrates differently — slower, denser, compressed information. Deep reading costs: headache behind left eye, metallic taste, hand tremors. Gets worse with repeated use. Surface reads are easy (walking, talking). Deep reads replace the reader's senses entirely — the above world recedes.
- **Branch work (Ch 3):** Vaelen can redirect Amarvel flow by "leaning into" a gradient — creating a signal of need that redirects resources. Not commanding — coaxing. Like pushing a boulder uphill. The Amarvel responds to the language of need: pressure, direction, warmth. Results: can raise ground temperature, reactivate capillaries, restore flow to abandoned branches. But it's temporary — the underlying pull will reassert.
- **The downstream pull (Ch 3):** Something south-southwest, 40+ miles, drawing steadily on the trunk line. The Amarvel redistributes toward it because the Amarvel feeds all demand equally — doesn't distinguish between a community's need and a deficit that will swallow everything. Directional, sustained, hungry.
- **The anomaly — what Vaelen found (Ch 3):** NOT in the Amarvel itself. In the SILENCE BETWEEN the Amarvel's pulses. A texture in the gaps — like a room where someone has just left. Something filling the space the Amarvel retreats from, a simulation so precise that the Amarvel's own systems process it as real. The instruments can't detect it. But the girl felt it through her bare feet. Whatever is happening is UPSTREAM of the Amarvel — it's the thing making the Amarvel retreat, not a product of the retreat.
- **Drenna's insight (Ch 3):** "Deeper doesn't mean more distance. It means a different way of listening." This unlocked the anomaly discovery — Vaelen stopped pushing through the roots and sat still, present, and felt the wrongness in the silence between pulses.

## 6. VOICE & STYLE NOTES

**POV structure:** Multi-POV, Martin convention. Each chapter titled with character name. Deep third person — everything filtered through the POV character's specific subjectivity.
**Caeleth voice (Ch 1):** Lives in his wanting, his nervousness, his inability to name what's wrong. Longer sentences, more metaphor, more atmospheric. The world is mysterious through his eyes.
**Drenna voice (Ch 2):** Lives in her hands, her observations, her assessments. Shorter sentences. More physical, more concrete. Less interiority — she doesn't analyze her feelings, she acts and the reader infers. The world is specific through her eyes — she names things, she counts, she files. Warmth buried under strategy. Humor dry and rare.
**Vaelen voice (Ch 3):** Fast, restless, always processing. His mind ingests everything automatically — people, rooms, soil, weather. He notices Drenna's boots, Caeleth's breathing pattern, Sorvyn's sleep posture. The control is visible from inside — choosing warmth, producing the right smile, being aware of the awareness. But underneath the management: raw sensory overwhelm when reading the Amarvel, and a love for the network that he can't contain. His one unguarded space. Contradiction: the most controlled person in the cast has the most uncontrollable inner experience.
**Style model:** Martin. Deep interiority. Sensory texture. Slower pace. Fewer details, more time inside each. The reader's unease built through the POV character's inability to name what's wrong.
**Prose voice:** Literary epic fantasy. Sentences that breathe. Metaphors drawn from the physical world (campfires, stone, water, furniture). Interior monologue woven through observation, not separated from it.
**Dialogue:** Rough, interrupted, people talking past each other. Humor through character, not wit. Jorath's money complaints reveal world mechanics. Sorvyn's declarations are plain and unperformative.
**Ethura on the page:** Never named. Never explained. Shown through sensory wrongness that the POV character can't articulate. The reader feels it before they understand it. Fewer details, more time inside each one. [Ch 2] Also shown through the father — the horror of a functioning bond with nobody at the other end.
**Vaelen's ambiguity:** Shown through micro-moments — the late-arriving smile, the shadow on his face, the mechanical quality of his network-reading. The reader likes him AND is slightly uneasy. Both feelings are valid. [Ch 2] Through Drenna's eyes: genuinely gifted, ignorant of his value, a man who defers to institutions that don't deserve it. She respects him and is frustrated by him simultaneously.
**Physical descriptions:** Woven into first encounters naturally. Specific and characterful (not police reports). What the POV character notices first reveals the POV character as much as the person described.

## 7. WRITING RULES (Learned from Mistakes)

*These rules exist because we broke them. Each one is a scar from a specific error.*

### Show, Never Interpret
**NEVER explain what a gesture, look, or silence means.** No "the look said:" or "the nod meant:" followed by a paragraph of interpretation. Describe the physical action. Stop. If the meaning isn't clear from the action alone, the action needs rewriting, not annotation. Trust the reader.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Maret's look described with a full paragraph of what it "contained."*

### No Thematic Vocabulary in Prose
**NEVER use words from our planning discussions in character voice.** "Perform," "institution," "authenticity," "identity," "structure," "scaffolding" — these are analytical terms for US, not words characters think in. Drenna doesn't think "I'm performing belonging." She thinks about the specific thing she's doing. The reader concludes the theme. The characters live it.
*Origin: Ch 2 — "perform" and "institution" used repeatedly in Drenna's POV.*

### Characters Contradict Themselves
**Every POV character must show at least one moment per chapter that contradicts their dominant trait.** Drenna must be unstrategic somewhere. Vaelen ungraceful. Sorvyn doubting. Jorath generous. Dimension comes from contradiction. A character who is only one thing is a thesis, not a person.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Drenna written as pure strategic calculator, unidimensional.*

### Specific Emotions, Not Expected Ones
**Before writing an emotional beat, ask: is this how THIS person would feel, or how MOST people would feel?** If "most people," it's wrong. Characters are specific. Drenna doesn't feel shame about her father — she carries him as proof of her resilience. Default psychology is lazy psychology.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Drenna written as ashamed of her father, when she'd actually frame it as evidence of how exceptional she is.*

### Same Trait, New Expression
**A character trait should never be expressed the same way twice.** Find new behaviors that reveal the same underlying quality. Jorath loves money — show it through a bet, a negotiation, a refusal, a surprising generosity. Not the same bit repeated. If a character moment feels familiar, it's a repeat. Change the action.
*Origin: Ch 1-2 — Jorath's coin-counting repeated without variation.*

### Professional Infrastructure Exists
**Before writing logistics (lodging, transport, meals), ask: what would the professional/institutional version be?** Practitioners on deployment have protocols, infrastructure, perks. They don't sleep in the client's house. The world has hotels, guest quarters, established systems. Think like a consultant, not a guest.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Team lodged at elder's house instead of practitioner quarters.*

### Characters Don't Have Reader Knowledge
**Characters can only know what their experience and culture gives them.** The systemic Ethura pattern is unprecedented — no character has our vocabulary for it. People reach for familiar frameworks (myths, generational stories, folk explanations) that don't quite fit. The gap between their explanation and reality is where tension lives.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Writing as if characters understood the systemic phenomenon when they shouldn't.*

### No Jacket-Blurb Sentences
**If a sentence could work as a book review quote, it's probably summarizing instead of showing.** "He read suffering in the earth the way she read suffering in communities" is a blurb, not prose. Cut it. Let the reader write that sentence in their own mind after accumulating the evidence across scenes.
*Origin: Ch 2 — neat literary symmetry replacing lived experience.*

### Pre-Chapter Checklist
**Before writing each chapter, review:** (1) the outline for agreed-on scenes/beats, (2) the Story Bible for continuity, (3) these writing rules, (4) any discussion notes for specific details. Don't rely on memory. Check the record.
*Origin: Ch 1 — Mirathyn joke scene forgotten because it wasn't checked against discussion notes.*

### Problem Solvers, Not Bureaucrats
**These characters are field practitioners. They DO things.** They fix nodes, walk land, talk to families, read the Amarvel. Journals and documents exist but should feel incidental, not defining. If a character's main activity in a scene is writing or filing, something is wrong. Show them in the dirt.
*Origin: Ch 2 — Drenna and Vaelen felt like people who write reports all day rather than people who solve problems in the field.*

### No Analytical Vocabulary in Prose
**Words like "frameworks," "perform," "institution," "structure," "scaffolding," "unidimensional" belong to our planning discussions, not to character voices.** If a word sounds like it came from a literary analysis essay rather than a person's thoughts, cut it. Characters think in concrete, specific, sensory terms.
*Origin: Ch 2 — "frameworks" overused alongside "perform" and "institution."*

### Drenna Has No Political Document
**NEVER write Drenna with a secret political document, separate binding, unlabeled notebook, or any physical prop representing her ambition.** Her political arc lives in how she thinks, acts, reads rooms, and chooses her moves. She's a field practitioner, not a pamphleteer. Her ambition is behavioral, not written. This has been corrected multiple times — do not resurrect it.
*Origin: Repeated correction across multiple drafts. Zombie idea that kept returning.*

### Drenna Always Shows Both
**Every Drenna POV scene must show the compassion AND the ambition simultaneously.** She feels AND she files. She grieves AND she assesses what the grief is worth. She shares personal truth AND knows exactly what that honesty does to a relationship. Neither cancels the other — both are real, always, at the same time. If a Drenna scene shows only compassion, the ambition is missing. If it shows only calculation, the humanity is missing. She is Tabata Amaral: genuinely from the favela, genuinely cares about education, genuinely plans to be president, and knows exactly what her origin story is worth in the right room. The reader sees both and decides how to feel. That tension IS the character.
*Origin: Drenna was being written as "compassionate woman who is also strategic" rather than "ambitious woman whose compassion is real." The ambition is primary. The compassion is also primary. Neither is the mask for the other.*
