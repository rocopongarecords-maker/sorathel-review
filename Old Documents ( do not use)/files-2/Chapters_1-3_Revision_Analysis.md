## CHAPTER 1 (CAELETH) — MINIMAL CHANGES

The chapter is strong. Caeleth's voice works, the wrongness seeds are planted (child's smile, flowers, water), the team dynamics land. Most of our new decisions don't require Ch1 changes because they play out in later episodes.

### KEEP AS-IS:
- Opening carriage sequence (great entry)
- Team dynamics (Vaelen warmth, Sorvyn faith, Jorath money)
- Korveth description and Hathren bond
- Drenna's entrance and assessment
- Wrongness signs (child's smile, flowers, water)
- Torven's grandmother story
- Drenna standing east at night
- Everything about Caeleth's wanting to belong

### SURGICAL FIXES NEEDED:

1. **Jorath resourcefulness beat.** Currently he only counts money and audits Hathren. He needs ONE moment of creative problem-solving — finding a workaround, improvising something, being clever in a way that isn't just financial. Maybe during the deployment setup at the node: he rigs something with the equipment, finds a faster way to set up the field station, solves a practical problem sideways. Brief — 2-3 sentences. Shows he's resourceful, not just a bean counter.

2. **Caeleth "beloved" seed.** The chapter already has his wanting to belong and his perceptiveness. But we need one more specific moment that makes the reader LOVE him — maybe a small kindness toward someone (the child on the wall? helping with something unprompted?), or a moment of unexpected humor that's endearing rather than awkward. Something the reader will remember in Episode 4 when he sits down and doesn't get up.

3. **Drenna's ambition in behavior (not just observation).** Ch1 shows Drenna as competent and direct. But the Tabata dual-nature — the feeling AND filing — could be slightly sharper. When she presents her journal data to Vaelen, the prose could include a brief beat where she assesses HIS reaction — not just sharing information, but reading how he receives it, filing his response as data about what kind of ally he might be. One sentence. The reader picks it up or doesn't.

## CHAPTER 2 (DRENNA) — MODERATE CHANGES

This chapter is good but has the most alignment issues with our new decisions. The father visit is powerful. The western fields scene works. But Drenna's interiority needs to be more actively strategic, and we need to plant the regional body departure.

### KEEP AS-IS:
- Father visit sequence (devastating, perfectly calibrated)
- Maret interaction
- Jorath's drinking bet with the boy (great character beat)
- Drenna's observation about the "gap" 
- The western fields scene with Vaelen
- "Nobody is coming" ending
- Mirathyn stalk dream

### SURGICAL FIXES NEEDED:

1. **Drenna assessing the team's political value.** In the breakfast scene, when she watches Jorath's bet and feels "envy" of his ease — this beat is good but could be sharper. Add a brief thought where she assesses the team's usefulness to her trajectory. Not cold — just the way her mind always works. Something like: she notes that Vaelen's pattern-reading ability could change the conversation about the frontier IF he understood what his readings meant politically. She's already thinking about how his gift serves a larger purpose. She's not using him — she's seeing what he can't see about himself. This is the Tabata dual nature: genuine respect for his ability AND awareness of its strategic value.

2. **Plant the regional body.** Somewhere in Drenna's interiority — maybe during the night scene at the end — a brief reference to what's waiting for her after this deployment. Not the full plan. Just a thought: something about the next assignment, or a person who arranged something for her, or the awareness that this field rotation is a step toward a specific destination. The reader files it. When she departs in Ch4, it doesn't come from nowhere.

3. **Drenna's father visit — add the strategic filing.** The visit is emotionally powerful. But per our Tabata rule, she should be feeling AND filing. After the visit, walking back, she's already thinking about what her father's story means as EVIDENCE — not for a document (Writing Rule: no political document prop), but in terms of how she'll use this experience. She already has the line about the resemblance between her father and the community. Sharpen it: she notices the parallel AND she knows this parallel is politically explosive. If she can name the connection between substance abuse and systemic network failure, she has something nobody else has. She doesn't write it down. She carries it.

4. **Jorath resourcefulness.** The drinking bet is great for his personality but it's still about money/negotiation. One beat elsewhere in the chapter where he solves a practical problem creatively. Maybe during the field work: he improvises equipment, jury-rigs something, finds a shortcut that Drenna or Vaelen wouldn't have thought of because they're too focused on the network. Brief.

5. **The "building a case" language.** Remove or replace any instance of Drenna "building a case" or "building evidence." She's not a researcher. She's a person who sees patterns and knows their political value. The language should be about SEEING and KNOWING, not collecting and building.

## CHAPTER 3 (VAELEN) — MINIMAL CHANGES

This chapter is the strongest of the three. Vaelen's interiority is rich, the deep reading sequence is compelling, the anomaly discovery is perfectly calibrated. Very few changes needed.

### KEEP AS-IS:
- Night waking and node reading
- Three-layer Amarvel description (trunk bass, branch midrange, capillary chatter)
- Downstream pull discovery
- Morning conversation with Sorvyn
- Branch work with Caeleth ("bees behind a wall", "it's beautiful")
- Drenna's insight ("deeper doesn't mean more distance")
- The midnight anomaly discovery
- The shaking hands, the metallic taste, the physical cost
- "Almost exactly" ending

### SURGICAL FIXES NEEDED:

1. **Creature rapport seed (warging).** During the branch work scene or another moment, add a brief beat where Vaelen interacts with or notices a creature — not a bonded one, but a wild or semi-wild creature near the settlement. The creature responds to his presence in a way that's slightly unusual. Not magical — subtle. Like a wild animal that should be wary but isn't. Or a creature at the settlement's edge that turns toward him when he's deep in a read, as if it can feel what he's doing. He notices but doesn't think about it. The reader notices. This is the Season 3 warging seed — planted so gently it's invisible until later.

2. **Drenna insight scene — add her political assessment.** When Drenna gives him the "deeper doesn't mean more distance" insight, the current scene is focused on Vaelen's reaction. Add one line from Vaelen's perspective where he reads something in Drenna beyond the intellectual partnership — maybe he notices she's already thinking about what this discovery means beyond the field. He files it without naming it. The reader sees: he's aware she has a larger agenda, and he doesn't mind. Or he does mind but doesn't examine it.

3. **Vaelen's awareness of the ceiling.** The chapter shows his competence and his cost. But the ceiling — the sense that he's too good for the Solvarei and not brave enough to leave — isn't explicit yet. One internal beat. Maybe after the branch work succeeds and the western fields warm up: a moment of satisfaction that immediately curdles. "He'd done this. He could feel the difference in the soil. And in a week they'd move to the next community and do it again, and the week after that, and the pattern would continue until he died or broke or left." Something brief that shows the trap.
