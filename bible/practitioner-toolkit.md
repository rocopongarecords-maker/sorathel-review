## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

**The Amarvel is the operating system of reality in Sorathel.** It connects to everything: the ground, the buildings (root-grown architecture), the creatures (bonded), the agriculture, the climate, the light (bioluminescence), sound (root vibration), and people's physical state (ambient connection). A practitioner who manipulates the network isn't tending a garden — they're **conducting reality**.

## WHAT PRACTITIONERS CONTROL THROUGH THE NETWORK

### The Physical Environment
Soil temperature, moisture, structural integrity. When a practitioner works a junction, the ground warms. Cold spots disappear. Frost retreats visibly. The street you're standing on responds to a person's will.

### Root-Grown Architecture
Buildings in Sorathel are partly grown from the network. A practitioner can reinforce walls, seal doorways, reshape passages. Root material responds to practitioner direction — slowly for major structures, fast enough in emergencies to be dramatic. Buildings BREATHE with the network. When the network weakens, architecture sags. When a practitioner restores it, buildings straighten visibly.

### Creatures
Through the network, a practitioner can calm a panicking herd, steady a bonded creature's state, reinforce or restore a weakening bond. Creature influence through the network is standard practitioner skill. Vaelen does it at extraordinary depth (the warging seed).

### Agriculture and Living Things
Crops, plants, flowers — all respond to network health. A practitioner restoring a junction can make flowers open, fruit ripen, grain sprout. When the network fails, produce looks ripe but tastes flat. Food is WRONG. Restoring the network restores the life in it.

### Light (Bioluminescence)
The network's bioluminescence IS the city's lighting. Street markers, building interiors, the ambient glow of a healthy district. A practitioner can brighten, dim, direct, or reshape the light. When they work, the light RESPONDS — surging, shifting, tracing their attention through the root channels. The most dramatic visual: a practitioner restoring a junction and the streetlights coming on, block by block, warm gold spreading through the walls.

### Sound (Root Vibration)
The roots vibrate. A healthy network HUMS — a bass note under the street, ambient resonance that carries conversations, fills spaces. Practitioners can produce tones, rhythms, warning signals. In distressed areas, sound dampens. When restored, the hum returns — conversations carry normally, the district sounds alive again.

### People (Ambient Connection)
Through the network, a practitioner can influence the physical state of people on connected ground. Not mind control — but calming nervous systems, steadying heartbeats, soothing pain. People near a working practitioner FEEL something: warmth, calm, rightness. Or, when things go wrong: nausea, vertigo, cold. Practitioners can also READ people on connected ground — stress levels, physical condition, deception (heartrate spikes).

## THE DIAGNOSTIC TOOLKIT — READING

### Surface Read
Quick. No cost. The equivalent of taking someone's pulse. Current state of a junction or channel: healthy, stressed, failing, dead. Any trained practitioner can do this.

### Pattern Read (Vaelen's Gift)
Vaelen's extraordinary ability. He separates thirty, forty live signals simultaneously — trade data, creature bonds, communication channels, deep trunk pulse — the way a conductor hears individual instruments in an orchestra. Other practitioners identify five signals in the time Vaelen separates thirty. This processing SPEED is what makes him a star.

### Forensic Read
Vaelen's signature. He touches a junction and sees its HISTORY — not just current state but months or years of activity. Who passed through. What changed and when. The district as it was a year ago (thriving, bright, warm) versus now (dimming, cooling, sagging). Played as a time-lapse of collapse. He can pinpoint the exact moment a failure started.

His legendary read: 18 months of node data from a 3-second unfocused stare at 200 meters. Other practitioners do forensic reads — Vaelen does them at a speed and distance that makes experienced practitioners stop and watch.

### Making the Invisible Visible (Projection)
A practitioner can make the network's activity visible to non-practitioners. The bioluminescence responds to their attention, traces flow patterns, highlights disruptions. Vaelen can project a forensic read outward — the ground lights up, healthy flows in warm gold, disruptions dark, the south-southwest draw a cold blue thread pulling capacity away. The client sees their own infrastructure mapped in living light beneath their feet. Most practitioners can't do this while simultaneously reading — it requires holding two operations at once.

### Reading People Through the Ground
When someone stands on connected network, Vaelen can read their physical state. Stress. Sleep deprivation. Whether they're lying (heartrate spikes). The commercial operator claiming the last practitioner caused the cascade — Vaelen reads his body through the ground and knows he's leaving something out. Walking lie detector on connected soil.

### Deep Read (Free-Diving)
Full immersion. The practitioner's senses are replaced entirely — blind, deaf, their consciousness spread across miles of root system. A vast living geography: rivers of energy, junctions like cities from above, the trunk signal like an ocean current. Beautiful and dangerous. You can get lost — lose the way back to your body. The headaches, metallic taste, tremors are the body's distress signals saying "come back." Deep reading is free-diving: the deeper you go, the more you see, the harder it is to surface. Vaelen goes deeper than anyone.

## THE INTERVENTION TOOLKIT — FIXING

### Restoring / Conducting
The primary work. A practitioner doesn't "redirect flow" — they **bring a failing area back to life**. The bioluminescence surges. Buildings straighten. Creatures settle. Temperature normalizes. Food tastes right. The streetlights come on. The hum returns. The practitioner stands at a junction and the world rearranges itself around them.

This is the spectacular work. The thing that makes civilians stop and watch. The thing that makes people want to be practitioners.

**The cost:** Every second of conducting burns the practitioner's cognitive resources. Headache behind the left eye. Metallic taste. Tremors. They're supplementing the network's failing signal with their own energy. The deeper and longer the restoration, the higher the cost.

### Anchoring (Emergency Hold)
When a junction is about to cascade and there's no time for a permanent fix, the practitioner plugs THEMSELVES in. They hold the junction stable with their own energy — a human buttress. Keeping channels open, bonds active, flow moving. Visibly strained: sweating, pale, trembling. They can hold it for twenty minutes to an hour depending on skill. It buys time. It's the equivalent of compressing an artery with bare hands while someone preps the real fix.

### Growing (Bypass / New Connection)
The practitioner stimulates the network to grow a new connection — around a damaged channel, between two separated systems, into new territory. Root tendrils extend under the practitioner's hands: visible, alive, seeking. The network decides whether to accept or reject the growth — wrong soil, wrong angle, incompatible channel means the root pulls back. It takes hours. The practitioner maintains contact, feeding the growth with attention. Beautiful to watch: a living system building infrastructure because someone asked it to.

### Severing (Amputation)
Cutting a dead or cascading channel to protect the rest. The network REACTS — bioluminescence flares, surrounding channels compensate wildly, creatures on the severed channel feel it instantly. Everything downstream of the cut loses connection: communities, creature bonds, infrastructure. The practitioner chooses what to sacrifice. The moral weight IS the drama.

### Flushing (Clearing a Blockage)
Driving a high-intensity pulse through a clogged or compromised channel. The bioluminescence surges, the ground trembles, the channel clears with a visible rush of energy. Fast, dramatic. The risk: if the channel is weaker than the blockage, the pulse BURSTS the channel. Permanent damage. Clients love it (fast results). Careful practitioners avoid it (catastrophic failure mode).

### Rebonding (Creature Work)
Restoring or strengthening a creature bond. One hand on the creature, one on the root surface. The practitioner becomes a bridge — feeling both sides: the creature's state (fear, confusion, wildness) and the network's signal (weakening, thinning). They match the signals — tune them into alignment. Intimate, physical, difficult. The creature has to trust you. When it works: the creature settles, the bond hums, the handler exhales.

## WHAT GOES WRONG (CASCADE FAILURES)

A botched intervention doesn't just "fail." The physical world reacts:
- The ground cracks. Root channels rupture visibly.
- Buildings sag or buckle — root-grown walls losing structural integrity in seconds.
- Creatures in the area panic — bonds disrupted, signals scrambled. Handlers get thrown or bitten.
- People feel it: wave of nausea, vertigo, a chill that isn't temperature.
- Bioluminescence dies — the block goes dark.
- Sound deadens — the hum cuts out, conversations fall into silence.
- Temperature drops — a warm district goes cold in minutes.

The last practitioner who tried to fix the Velanthra junction caused exactly this. Three handlers hurt. A merchant's cargo creature lost eight months of route-training in a second. The client has been afraid to let anyone touch the junction since.

## SKILL HIERARCHY

### Branch-Level (Solvarei)
Work at the community level. Restore junctions, conduct local areas, read local histories, manage creature bonds. This is what most trained practitioners do. Vaelen is extraordinary at this level — faster, deeper, more precise than anyone.

### Trunk-Level (Amarith)
Work at the systemic level. Manage the deep currents that connect regions, regulate continent-scale flows, read trunk-level patterns that determine the health of entire territories. The difference between branch and trunk is not degree — it's CATEGORY. A branch practitioner reading a trunk signal is like a swimmer looking at the ocean. The Amarith Practitioner does this routinely.

### The Gap
Vaelen has the talent for trunk-level work. He doesn't have the institutional access, the training, or the willingness to reach for it. The ceiling is the Amarith — looking outward, not upward. His forensic reads and processing speed are trunk-adjacent. The Practitioner in Velanthra recognizes this in the pause before he turns back to his work. "Interesting for his tier."

## TEAM OPERATIONS

The Solvarei deploys in teams because complex interventions require multiple practitioners working in concert:

- **Lead practitioner (Vaelen):** Conducts the intervention. Reads, decides, executes.
- **Trunk monitor (Sorvyn):** Watches the deep signal for instability. The early warning system. If the trunk shifts during an intervention, the lead needs to know immediately.
- **Physical infrastructure (Jorath):** Manages the hardware — junction housings, root channels, physical structures. The network is alive but it grows through physical substrate. Someone needs to ensure the substrate can handle what the practitioner is doing.
- **Creature/bond monitor (Caeleth):** Reads creature bond stability during interventions. The living barometer. When bonds thin, the practitioner is pushing too hard.
- **Political/client management (Drenna):** Not at the junction. In the room where the deployment is negotiated, the costs explained, the results sold. The Solvarei runs on reputation and referrals. Someone has to manage the human side.
