## THE MISSION SPINE

**What the team is told:** Standard assignment. A Mirathyn farming community on the frontier needs practical help — yields declining, creatures behaving erratically, network nodes failing. Fix the problems, deliver results, move on to the next assignment: a network junction further along the route toward the trading port of [name TBD]. Standard operational tempo.

**What actually happens:** At an intermediate community on their journey toward the junction, the team learns that a previous Solvarei team passed through heading deeper into the frontier — and hasn't been heard from since. Nobody mentioned this in the briefing. The team CHOOSES to investigate. The routine mission becomes a rescue. They find the missing team — Hushed, along with an entire settlement. Among the wreckage: the missing team's fragmentary notes, showing they discovered something in the network before the Ethura took them. Something that connects to what Vaelen has been sensing.

**What the reader experiences:** A standard fantasy adventure that suddenly gets dark. The bank heist that reveals the Epstein files. "We thought this was one kind of story. It's another kind entirely."

## THE TEAM

**Vaelen** — Long-range reader. Goes deep. The one the commander sends because "you'll see what others won't." His training reshaped his nervous system more than most — he interfaces with the network at a depth that other practitioners find unnerving. Magnetic, funny, generous. The reader falls in love.

**Sorvyn** (inspired by Felipe) — Network surgeon. Structural specialist. When a node needs cutting, merging, rerouting — Sorvyn is the one with the steady hands. Started as Vaelen's shadow. Now finding his own footing. His contentment with the ceiling is visible: he does precise, essential work and doesn't chase more.

**Doumm** — Creature negotiator. The gift with living things. When a bond is breaking between creature and human, Doumm enters the space between them and translates. Also the team's emergency bonder — when a panicked, unbonded creature needs a bridge connection fast, Doumm is the one who can do it without getting hurt. The heart of the team. Leave coming up. Plans. The ordinary future.

**Jorath** (inspired by Johny) — Extraction specialist. The one who goes in after people. When someone is lost inside the network — a practitioner who went too deep, a creature that got trapped — Jorath goes in and brings them back. He's been inside bad network spaces that should have killed him. That's the source of both his damage and his dark humor. The team's ballast.

**Junior practitioner** (name TBD) — Newer. Eager. Reader surrogate. Asks questions the reader wants answered. Still getting used to the physical cost of network engagement — comes out of routine readings pale and shaky while the veterans barely blink.

**Deployment creatures** — Professional bonds. Functional, reliable, lacking the intimacy of generational bonds. Each practitioner's creature amplifies their specialty: Vaelen's extends his perception range, Sorvyn's steadies node structures during surgery, Doumm's communicates with other creatures, Jorath's navigates inside network spaces.

## DRENNA'S TRACK

Drenna arrives as part of Vaelen's deployment — the Solcarin placed her on an elite rotation, cycling high-potential trainees through the best deployment teams. The team knows this. Simultaneously a rookie and someone with more institutional backing than anyone present.

After the initial deployment (Episode 1), she rotates to her own separate assignment. Her POV chapters then run parallel.

**Her situation:** First-year Solvarei. Elite Solcarin graduate from the Hearthlands institution. Chose the frontier posting — CV building. The Hearthlands Solcarin gave her extraordinary training — her nervous system interfaces with the network at institutional-elite level. She's technically more credentialed than anyone on Vaelen's team except Vaelen himself. The gap between her training and her field experience is the source of friction.

**Her father:** Hushed by heavy Mirathyn. Creature still waits outside. He will be killed during the surge or its immediate aftermath.

## ZETHAEL'S THREAD

Same generation as Vaelen. On his own deployments. Appears in the three-way scene (Episode 1), his own POV chapter (Episode 2), and his goodbye (Episode 3).

**The technique:** Same methodology, radically faster, different tools. Removes the dependency on channeling Amarwen's power. Works through the network's own responsiveness. Where the standard practitioner goes inside a node and channels Amarwen's energy to force stability, Zethael goes inside and works WITH the node's own response — nudging, reading the reaction, nudging again. Five cycles in the time others complete one.

**The Ohn discovery (seeded):** In Episode 2, inside a dead zone where Amarwen's network has collapsed, his trained nervous system responds to something that isn't Amarwen. The seed of everything.

## CHAPTER 1 — VAELEN: "The Arriving"

**Opens in motion.** Vaelen and his Solvarei team traveling through frontier territory. He feels the network through his body — not metaphor, physical sensation. His training changed his nervous system. The ground beneath him carries information the way air carries sound. This region's rhythm is tired. Sluggish. Like a heartbeat that's slowing.

His creature walks beside him, slightly ahead — reading the path, extending Vaelen's perception range. Where Vaelen feels the network's rhythm, the creature feels its edges. Together they perceive a fuller picture than either can alone. The bond is professional — not generational, not deep — but functional and practiced. They've worked together long enough for this.

**The team as individuals — introduced through their specialties:**

- **Sorvyn:** Walking beside Vaelen. Carrying his tools — whatever the Sorathel equivalent of surgical instruments is. Specific, personal, well-maintained. He touches them the way a craftsman touches good tools. Easy familiarity with Vaelen. A comment showing shared history. When they pass a node that's structurally awkward — grown at a bad angle, putting stress on the pathway — Sorvyn clocks it the way a carpenter clocks a crooked beam. "That'll need cutting eventually." Professional reflex.

- **Doumm:** Doesn't feel the network the way Vaelen does. Feels the CREATURES. Walking past a field where farming creatures are working, Doumm reads the animals — their body language, their bond-state, their mood. "That one's unhappy. See how it keeps shifting away from the node? The bond's strained. Not broken — strained. Something changed." A diagnostic that comes from understanding living things, not network signals. Mentions leave. Plans. The ordinary future.

- **Jorath:** Dark humor about a past extraction. "Last time I went in after someone, the network space had collapsed to about this wide" — holds hands close together — "and the practitioner was wedged between two root structures screaming about how he'd rather die in there than let me see him cry." The team laughs. The junior practitioner isn't sure if it's funny or terrifying. Jorath's creature is different from the others — built for navigating INSIDE the network. Compact, agile, with senses oriented toward enclosed spaces.

- **The junior practitioner:** Newer. Still adjusting to the physical reality of the practice. On the journey, they touch a roadside node to practice basic reading and come away slightly dizzy. Vaelen notices, says nothing — every practitioner goes through this. Their body is still learning to interface. The reader sees: this practice costs something, and the veterans are so used to the cost they don't notice it anymore.

- **Deployment creatures:** Each one different. Matched to their practitioner's specialty. The reader sees living partnerships, not pets or tools.

**Arrival at the Mirathyn farming community.** The reader's first full look at Sorathel:

- Farming creatures working nodes alongside families. GENERATIONAL bonds — creatures that have been with this family for lifetimes. The depth of connection visible in how they move together. The team's professional bonds look thin by comparison.
- Mirathyn growing from the nodes. A crop. A product. The farmer's pride in their cultivation — this isn't just agriculture, it's craft. Like a vineyard.
- A messenger creature arriving late from another community. Someone remarks on the delay — this used to be instantaneous.
- A farming creature hesitating near a node. Backing away. The farmer watching with grief. Doumm sees it instantly: "The creature isn't refusing. It's afraid. Something about that node changed."
- Children playing with younger creatures. The next generation of bonds forming naturally.

**The work begins.** The Solvarei embed. Vaelen assesses with the lead farmer. The farmer describes the problems in farmer's terms — yields bad, creatures agitated, something feels wrong. Vaelen translates into practitioner's terms — he kneels, places his hands on the ground near the primary node, and READS. His creature takes position. His eyes shift — the focus moving to something the farmer can't see.

The reader's first look at pattern-reading: not passive sensing. Vaelen's body is engaged — slight movements, micro-adjustments, his breathing changing as he interfaces deeper. It takes minutes. When he surfaces, he's slightly pale. Drinks water. Then assigns the team:

"Three nodes underperforming — one has a growth conflict with the neighbor's network, one has a blockage in the eastern pathway, and one has a structural crack that's leaking signal. Sorvyn, the crack is yours. Doumm, two creatures are refusing — find out why. Jorath, the blockage might need hands inside. I'll take the growth conflict."

Each person moves to their assignment. The reader sees: a team deploying with specific skills toward specific problems. Not generic "fixing" — each person doing something DIFFERENT.

**Drenna.** Elite Solcarin rotation. Vaelen clocks her credentials. She clocks his. She contributes an early observation — she reads the primary node differently from Vaelen. Not better, not worse — different. Her Hearthlands Solcarin training emphasized something his didn't. She sees the economic stress pattern where he sees the signal degradation. Two lenses on the same problem. Vaelen recalibrates. The team registers: this first-year sees things.

**The Mirathyn joke.** Doumm, working with the frightened creature near a node, makes a crack about heavy Mirathyn to the farmer. Casual, charming — Doumm's gift is putting people at ease while working. The team laughs. Drenna's jaw tightens. Nobody notices.

**Chapter ends:** Evening. The first day's work done. Vaelen alone near the primary node. He reaches out — not a formal reading, just resting his awareness on the network. Beautiful. Fragile. The song getting quieter. And underneath: something else. Faint. Wrong. New. Not part of the network's normal signals. Not damage. Not decay. Something that shouldn't be there.

The anomaly. The reader enters the mystery.

## CHAPTER 2 — DRENNA: "The Rotation"

**ON Vaelen's deployment.** Drenna's first POV. Her lens on the same situation — but she sees entirely different things.

**Watching practitioners work.** Drenna observes Sorvyn doing network surgery on the cracked node. From her Solcarin training, she knows the theory. She's never watched someone do it in the field. Sorvyn kneels at the node, his creature bracing the structure from the other side. He goes inside — hands on the node, eyes shifting, the focus change the reader now recognizes. Minute movements. Precise. His creature adjusting in response. Minutes pass. When he surfaces, the crack is sealed. He's tired but steady. A craftsman's satisfaction.

Drenna thinks: competent but slow. Her training suggests a faster technique. She doesn't say this. She files it. The reader sees: she might be right. She also has no field experience. The gap between knowing and doing is the thing she can't yet measure.

**Her assessment of the team.** Vaelen: gifted, charismatic, the team's center of gravity. His long-range readings are beyond anything she saw at the Solcarin — even her professors would be impressed. Sorvyn: steady hands, limited ambition. Doumm: extraordinary creature instincts but disorganized, too emotional. Jorath: damaged, reliable, the one you want in a crisis. The junior practitioner: irrelevant. She catalogues everyone in minutes. She's wrong about Doumm — "too emotional" is exactly what makes creature negotiation work. She won't understand this for a long time.

**Her actual skill.** She does a reading of her own — the economic and supply-chain dimensions of the farming community's problems. Her analysis adds something Vaelen missed. She presents it with precision. The team listens. She's not performing — she's delivering.

**Her father — a thread.** Working near a node, she sees a farming creature lying next to a man who doesn't respond. The creature nudging. The man still. Not Hushed — just old, sleeping, ordinary. But the image hits her. She turns away. Moves on. The reader catches the parallel.

**Her mentor connection.** Brief communication. "How's the rotation?" Strategic report. Institutional gossip exchanged. The transaction visible.

**Chapter ends:** Drenna receiving notice of her next rotation — separate deployment, different team, different part of the frontier. She looks at Vaelen's team with the eye of someone who's already leaving. She learned what she needed. Filed what's useful. The reader holds: same world, different lens.

## CHAPTER 3 — VAELEN: "The Work"

**THE PRACTICE SHOWCASE.** This is the chapter where the reader sees what Solvarei actually do — in variety, in detail, in cost.

**SEQUENCE 1 — Vaelen goes inside.** The growth conflict: two nodes competing for the same network section. The farmer's node is being starved by the neighbor's expansion. This can't be fixed from outside — Vaelen needs to go in and reroute the pathway.

He prepares. His creature takes position at the node's secondary access point. The reader sees the preparation — whatever physical setup is required. Not casual. Ritual-level attention.

He places both hands on the node. His creature engages from its position. And he goes IN.

What the farmer sees: Vaelen goes still. His breathing changes. His eyes are open but seeing something else. His creature mirrors his stillness — locked in, amplifying. Minutes pass. The farmer waits. This is what he's seen Solvarei do his whole life — the mysterious going-in that fixes things he can't fix himself.

What the READER sees (Vaelen's POV): he enters the network space. The reader's first look at what's inside. A luminous, organic environment — root structures like cathedral architecture, pathways of light, the living infrastructure of Amarwen's network made visible. Beautiful. Strange. The air (if it is air) hums with signal. His creature is with him — navigating, reading, extending his reach.

He finds the conflict — two root structures growing into each other, tangling, competing. The farmer's pathway is being choked. Vaelen has to reroute — gently separate the competing roots, redirect one without damaging the other. It requires PHYSICAL work inside the space — touching, guiding, using his trained intention to persuade living structures to move. The network responds to him because his nervous system speaks its language. But it doesn't obey — it cooperates or it doesn't. He has to read its responses, adjust, try again.

It works. The pathways separate. Signal flows restored to the farmer's node. Vaelen withdraws.

What the farmer sees: Vaelen surfaces. Pale. Slightly shaky. His creature presses close — stabilization through the bond. He sits for a moment. Drinks light Mirathyn. The color returns. Then: "Your pathway was tangled with your neighbor's irrigation extension. They didn't do it on purpose — the network grew that way. I've rerouted yours. The yield should normalize within a season."

The farmer stares. He's been blaming his creatures for two years.

**SEQUENCE 2 — Sorvyn's surgery.** The cracked node. Sorvyn doesn't go fully inside — he works at the threshold. Hands on the node, creature stabilizing the structure, his focus on the precise point of damage. Network surgery is surface work — you don't enter the space, you work the boundary. Like a surgeon who doesn't need to open the whole chest — precise, targeted intervention.

The reader sees: different technique, different specialty. Sorvyn's tools matter — whatever instruments he uses, they're extensions of his trained nervous system. The crack seals. The node stabilizes. Sorvyn surfaces with less visible cost than Vaelen — surgery is precise but shallow. Vaelen's deep work is the one that grinds you down.

**SEQUENCE 3 — Doumm's negotiation.** The two creatures refusing their nodes. Doumm's approach is completely different from Vaelen's or Sorvyn's. No going inside the network. Doumm works the BOND — sitting with the creature, reading its distress, interfacing with its perception to understand what it's sensing that the humans can't.

Doumm's creature approaches the frightened farming creature. Creature-to-creature communication — something practitioners can facilitate but not fully understand. The farming creature calms. Doumm sits with it. Patient. Minutes. Then: "It's not the node that's wrong. The creature is sensing something in the eastern section — something it doesn't recognize. It's afraid. The fear is disrupting the bond."

This connects to what Vaelen sensed — the anomaly is in the eastern section. The creature KNEW. Doumm translates what the creature can't articulate. The farmer approaches. His creature comes to him. The bond, strained for weeks, eases.

The farmer has tears in his eyes. Doumm doesn't know they've done something extraordinary. They just talked to an animal.

**SEQUENCE 4 — Jorath clears the blockage.** The eastern pathway. Jorath has to go partially inside — not deep like Vaelen, but into the pathway itself. A blockage: something tangled in the pathway, disrupting signal flow. Jorath and his creature enter. His creature navigates — built for enclosed spaces, reading the pathway's structure. Jorath finds the blockage — a knot of decayed root material clogging the channel. He clears it. Physical work in a confined space. His creature watches his back.

When he surfaces: "Cleared. Also, whatever's causing decay in there isn't normal aging. The material was healthy recently. Something killed it." He says this with the flat tone of someone reporting facts. The team registers: the blockage wasn't natural. Something is degrading the network faster than it should.

**Evening gathering.** Community and Solvarei together. Mirathyn shared — the light kind, celebration of the day's work. The beauty of the world at its most intimate. Vaelen feels it through his trained body — the network humming with the community's relief, the creature bonds easing, the Mirathyn warm and golden. Beautiful. Fragile.

**Chapter ends:** Vaelen alone. Reaches out to the network — not a formal reading, just resting his awareness. The song is quieter than it should be. Getting quieter. And underneath: the anomaly. Stronger tonight. Pulsing. The reader leans in.

## CHAPTER 4 — ZETHAEL: "The Friend"

**Surprise POV.** New voice. Direct, physical, kinetic. ADHD energy — thoughts jump, hands move, eyes catch everything.

**Zethael arrives.** Same Solvarei corps, different assignment nearby. Swings by to see Vaelen. Old friends. The one person Vaelen drops the polish around.

**The three-way scene.** Zethael, Vaelen, and Sorvyn sharing a meal. The ordinary moment that will be devastating in Season 3.

Zethael and Sorvyn: different energies. Sorvyn is settled — his tools cleaned and organized, his manner steady. Zethael is restless but outward — touching everything, asking questions about the community, remembering the farmer's name from a past visit.

**The technique's origin.** Through conversation, the reader learns: Vaelen and Zethael developed the experimental approach TOGETHER. Past deployment. Late night. "What if you didn't have to channel Amarwen's power inside the node? What if the node could do more of the work itself?" They tried it on a small scale — went inside together and instead of forcing the standard intervention, they nudged. Let the network respond. It showed promise.

Then Vaelen went back to standard operations. Zethael kept going.

**Zethael's practice — how it FEELS different.** He describes what happens when he goes inside a node his way. Standard practice: enter, channel Amarwen's energy through your nervous system, force the repair. You're the engine. Amarwen is the fuel. The node is passive.

Zethael's way: enter, nudge the node's own structure, read the response, nudge again. The node participates. "It WANTS to heal. You just have to stop pushing and let it show you how." Faster — five iterations in the time of one standard cycle. Less costly on the practitioner — because you're not channeling power, you're facilitating. But requires a different kind of attention: reading the node's responses in real time, adjusting constantly.

His creature is different too — during his interventions, the creature isn't just amplifying. It's a collaborator inside the space. Reading what Zethael can't, responding to the node's reactions from a different angle. The bond between them has deepened beyond professional parameters. He should reassign. He doesn't.

**The injustice trigger.** Zethael mentions a frustration — did extraordinary work. Went inside a node that had been failing for years, healed it his way in one session. What standard practice would have taken a week of channeled interventions. Result: nodes healthy, community thriving. Institutional response: commendation in a file. Method not acknowledged. "Continue standard approaches."

Vaelen sympathizes. Says: "Give it time. The system needs to see the pattern." Spoken from the position of someone the system has always worked for. Zethael hears it. Doesn't argue.

**Zethael in the community.** More present than anyone else. Sits with families. Plays with children. His creature — the one bonded beyond protocol — interacts with community creatures in a way that professional-bond creatures don't. Genuine. The community responds warmly. He doesn't notice the strategic value. The reader does.

**The goodbye.** Two friends. Next rotation. Casual. The reader doesn't know it's the last time they'll be peers.

**Chapter ends:** Zethael walking away. The reader thinks: I like him. I want to see him again.

## CHAPTER 5 — VAELEN: "The Diagnosis"

**The deeper problem.** Surface fixes working. But something underneath. Vaelen needs to do what he does best — go deep.

**Long-range reading.** Vaelen sits at the community's strongest node. His creature takes position — further away than the team has seen, extending the range. He interfaces deeply — not going inside the node but REACHING through it, stretching his perception across the regional network.

What the team sees: Vaelen sitting motionless. His creature locked in position. His breathing slowing to almost nothing. His face showing nothing — total immersion. Minutes. Many minutes. The junior practitioner gets nervous. Sorvyn murmurs: "He does this. Give him space." Doumm watches with concern — not professional concern, personal. Vaelen is IN there deep.

What the READER sees (Vaelen's POV): his perception expanding outward like sonar. The community's network, then the surrounding region, then further. Nodes like stars in a constellation. Pathways like rivers of light. He can feel communities he's never visited — their health, their stress, their creature populations.

And the pattern: the network stress isn't random. It's DIRECTIONAL. A wave of degradation propagating from somewhere deeper in the frontier toward the center. Node after node after node, each one slightly weaker than the last in a line pointing INWARD. Like watching dominoes falling in slow motion across the whole region.

He pushes further — toward the source. Deeper into the frontier. The signal gets weaker. The network thinner. His creature strains at the bond — they're at maximum range. The cost builds: his body shaking, his nose bleeding (or the Sorathel equivalent of physiological cost), his sense of the physical world dissolving.

At the very edge of his reach: the anomaly. STRONG. Not damage. Not decay. Something raw. Alive. Growing in the spaces where Amarwen's signal has already failed.

He pulls back. Hard. His creature recoils from the distance. Vaelen surfaces gasping. Falls to his hands. His vision is flat and grey. The world sounds wrong. Sorvyn and Doumm are beside him immediately. He needs heavy Mirathyn. The team watches him drink with the careful attention of people who know what just happened and are worried.

When he can speak: "It's not local. The degradation is coming from the deep frontier. There's a wave — directional, propagating inward. Every community between here and the source is going to feel this."

The junior practitioner: "How far did you go?"

Vaelen: "Too far."

**The team works the deeper problem with their specialties.** Sorvyn examines the structural data Vaelen brought back — the directional pattern confirms what he found inside the blocked pathway. The decay isn't natural. Doumm's creature translation adds another dimension — all the farming creatures are oriented away from the eastern section. They've been sensing the wave for weeks. The creatures knew before anyone.

**Drenna's insight (before her rotation).** Drenna connects the local pattern to institutional reports through her mentor network. The degradation pattern isn't just here — it's across the frontier. She delivers this with precision. Vaelen recalibrates.

**The anomaly.** Stronger. Connected to the directional pattern. The anomaly signal is strongest where the degradation originates. Vaelen can't tell if it's the cause or the response. The mystery deepens.

**Communication with the commander.** Reports escalated. "Consistent with reports from other regions." Next mission: follow the network toward the junction. Track the pattern.

Doumm grins: "Finally heading to the port."

**Chapter ends:** Successful deployment. Real results. But the directional pattern, and the cost of what Vaelen did to find it, linger. His hands are still trembling.

## CHAPTER 1 — VAELEN: "The Junction"

## CHAPTER 6 — ZETHAEL: "The Method"

**Zethael's deployment — the edge.** Completely different world. Small, isolated, quiet. A community on the margin. Zethael requested it.

**The technique in mechanical detail.** Standard approach: diagnose → identify → channel Amarwen's power → stabilize. One cycle.

Zethael's approach: diagnose → rapid first intervention (small, precise, not channeling Amarwen) → read the network's RESPONSE → adjust → intervene again → read → adjust → intervene. Five cycles in the time a standard practitioner completes one. Same foundational methodology. Different tools, different speed, different dependency.

He follows the standard method's foundations — admires them. But removes the dependency on Amarwen's channeled power. Works through the network's own responsiveness. Nudging rather than forcing. Letting the network participate in its own healing.

Result: nodes heal better. More organically. More sustainably. Not reinforced (like a cast) but healed (like bone knitting itself).

**His creature mirrors the approach.** During rapid cycles, the creature is a participant, not a tool. Reading, responding, adjusting alongside Zethael. The bond deepens through shared work — beyond protocol, beyond professional parameters. He should reassign the creature. He doesn't.

**THE OHN ACCIDENT — THE SEED.**

Working a section where Amarwen's network has already collapsed. Dead zone. His standard approach has nothing to work with — no network signal to iterate on. Frustration. He tries anyway — habit, stubbornness, the ADHD inability to accept "it can't be done."

He runs his rapid iteration cycle on the dead space itself. Nudging where there's nothing to nudge. Reading where there's nothing to read.

Something responds. Faint. Not Amarwen's warm, structured signal. Something else. Raw. Undifferentiated. Like static that almost — ALMOST — has a pattern.

His creature reacts — alert, not afraid. Reading something Zethael can barely sense.

Then it's gone. The dead space is dead again.

Zethael sits back. Tries to replicate it. Can't — not consistently. But something happened. He files it. Not in a report — in his own notes. "Network-dead zone, eastern section. Applied iterative method to void. Brief response — non-Amarwen signal. Creature confirmed. Unable to replicate. Revisit."

The reader files this too. They won't understand its significance until Season 3.

**The community.** Simple response: "It worked. More, please." They don't care about methodology.

**The institutional wall.** Previous report came back: "Interesting. Continue standard approaches." He reads it. Files it. Does the method anyway.

**Chapter ends:** Zethael looking at his readings. The standard approach reinforces. His approach heals. The difference is real, measurable, documented. Nobody with authority can see it. And there's something else now — that brief response from the void. Something he can't explain and can't replicate.

He doesn't know it yet. Nobody does. But he just made first contact with the force that will free civilization from dependency on a dying god.
