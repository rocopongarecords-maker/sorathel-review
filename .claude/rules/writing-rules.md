# Sorathel Writing Rules
*Each rule exists because we broke it. Each is a scar from a specific error.*

## 1. Show, Never Interpret
NEVER explain what a gesture, look, or silence means. No "the look said:" followed by interpretation. Describe the physical action. Stop. Trust the reader.

## 2. No Thematic Vocabulary in Prose
NEVER use: "perform," "institution," "authenticity," "identity," "structure," "scaffolding," "framework." These are analytical terms for planning, not character thoughts. Characters think in concrete, sensory terms.

## 3. Characters Contradict Themselves
Every POV character must show at least one moment per chapter that contradicts their dominant trait. Drenna unstrategic. Vaelen ungraceful. Sorvyn doubting. Jorath generous.

## 4. Specific Emotions, Not Expected Ones
Before writing an emotional beat: is this how THIS person would feel, or how MOST people would feel? Drenna doesn't feel shame about her father — she carries him as proof of resilience.

## 5. Same Trait, New Expression
A character trait should never be expressed the same way twice. Jorath loves money — show it through a bet, a negotiation, a refusal, a surprising generosity. Not the same bit repeated.

## 6. Professional Infrastructure Exists
Practitioners on deployment have protocols, infrastructure, perks. They don't sleep in the client's house. Think like a consultant, not a guest.

## 7. Characters Don't Have Reader Knowledge
Characters can only know what their experience and culture gives them. The systemic Ethura pattern is unprecedented — no character has our vocabulary for it.

## 8. No Jacket-Blurb Sentences
If a sentence could work as a book review quote, it's summarizing instead of showing. Cut it. Let the reader write that sentence in their own mind.

## 9. Pre-Chapter Checklist
Before writing each chapter, review: (1) outline for agreed beats, (2) Story Bible for continuity, (3) these Writing Rules, (4) discussion notes for specific details.

## 10. Problem Solvers, Not Bureaucrats
These characters are field practitioners. They DO things. If a character's main activity in a scene is writing or filing, something is wrong. Show them in the dirt.

## 11. No Analytical Vocabulary in Prose
Words like "frameworks," "perform," "institution," "structure," "scaffolding," "unidimensional" belong to planning, not character voices.

## 12. Drenna Has No Political Document
NEVER write Drenna with a secret document, separate binding, unlabeled notebook, or any physical prop representing her ambition. Her political arc lives in behavior. Zombie idea — do not resurrect.

## 13. Drenna Always Shows Both
Every Drenna scene must show compassion AND ambition simultaneously. She feels AND files. She grieves AND assesses what the grief is worth. Neither cancels the other. She is Tabata Amaral.

## GRRM Additions

## 14. Texture Scenes
Include at least one scene per episode that exists for world texture, not plot. A meal. A walk. A creature being tended. The reader needs to LIVE in Sorathel, not just visit during crises.

## 15. Libertarian Costs
When showing voluntary systems working, also show the costs. Freedom without structure means some people fall through. The frontier is structurally resilient but individually dangerous. Show both.

## 16. Magic Sensory Vocabulary
The Amarvel should be described with expanding sensory language — not the same metaphors repeated. Bass/midrange/chatter is established. Find new textures: temperature, taste, pressure, the feeling of standing near something vast.

## 17. Surface Behaviors Over Interior Analysis
Show what characters DO, not what they think about doing. If Vaelen is controlling his expression, describe the micro-movements — don't write "he controlled his expression." The reader should see the effort, not be told about it.
