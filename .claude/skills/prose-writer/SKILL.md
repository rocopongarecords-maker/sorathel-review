# Prose Writing Skill — Sorathel

## When to Use
Invoke this skill when writing or revising chapter prose.

## Pre-Flight Checklist
Before writing ANY prose:
- [ ] Read the episode outline for this chapter's agreed beats
- [ ] Read `bible/story-bible.md` for continuity
- [ ] Read the POV character's sheet in `characters/`
- [ ] Read `.claude/rules/writing-rules.md`
- [ ] Check previous chapter by this POV character for voice

## Chapter Targets
- 3,500–5,500 words per chapter
- Literary epic fantasy — sentences that breathe
- Metaphors from the physical world (campfires, stone, water, furniture)
- Interior monologue woven through observation, not separated from it
- Dialogue: rough, interrupted, people talking past each other
- Humor through character, not wit

## Writing Process
1. If a spec/outline exists for this chapter, follow its beats
2. Draft the full chapter in `drafts/`
3. Self-check against Writing Rules
4. Present to user for review
5. After approval, move to `episodes/ep##/`
6. Update Story Bible and character sheet
7. Log in `changelog.md`

## Voice Quick-Reference
- **Caeleth:** Long sentences, metaphor-rich, atmospheric. He lives in wanting and nervousness and inability to name what's wrong. The world is mysterious.
- **Drenna:** Short sentences. Physical, concrete. She names things, counts, files. Less interiority — she acts and the reader infers. Warmth buried under strategy.
- **Vaelen:** Fast, restless processing. Notices everything automatically. The control visible from inside — choosing warmth, producing the right response. But underneath: raw sensory overwhelm with the Amarvel. His one unguarded space.
- **Zethael:** Energetic, scattered. Ideas pile up faster than sentences. Talks with hands even in his own head. Direct. Frustrated with anything that moves slowly. Hyperfocus on whatever's caught his attention — the rest of the world disappears.

## Common Mistakes to Catch
- Analytical vocabulary in character voice ("perform," "institution," "framework")
- Interpreting gestures ("the look said:" → just describe the look)
- Drenna with a political document (NEVER)
- Repeated character beats without variation
- Jacket-blurb sentences
- Characters knowing things they shouldn't
- Describing the Ethura by name in prose
- Writing Vaelen as primarily a performer (he's gift-first — the warmth is real)
- Writing Drenna as primarily compassionate (she's ambition-first — the compassion is also real)
