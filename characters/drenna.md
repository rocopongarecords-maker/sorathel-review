# DRENNA — Rotation Practitioner

## Physical
- Twenty or twenty-one
- Long dark hair pulled back loosely, strands escaping
- Wide brown eyes, sharp cheekbones
- Mouth that looks perpetually considering something cutting
- Small/compact — not short, but precise and deliberate in movement
- New Solvarei field markings (stiff, insignia too bright)
- Thin frontier threadwork band at wrist (local plant dyes, family patterns)
- Boots caked with red-brown Mirathyn field soil

## The Tabata Amaral Core
- She is ambition-first. The compassion is also real. Neither is the mask for the other.
- Genuinely FROM the frontier but never fully OF it. She left the first chance she got.
- Every experience is simultaneously felt AND filed for what it means for her trajectory
- She doesn't think "I'm performing belonging." She thinks about the specific thing she's doing. The reader concludes the theme.
- Compassion without power is just a nicer way of watching people disappear.

## Background
- Grew up 40 minutes east of Torven's community
- Father started Mirathyn extract when Drenna was 10 — bond weakened from thinning node
- By 12, father in the chair. By 14, bond burned through — creature's lichen went dark
- Father hasn't spoken in years. Neighbor Maret maintains his dwelling
- Attended the most prestigious Solcarin (the Harvard one). Arrived with dirt under fingernails and frontier accent; left with top marks and a voice for any room
- The other students knew which fork to use. She learned to move through rooms without snagging.

## Competence
- Identified that node decline is NOT cyclical (contradicting last season's specialist)
- Noticed water tastes like nothing — and that people adapting to it is more concerning than the symptom
- Tracked identical decline patterns across 4 communities
- Connected the Mirathyn abuse / systemic decline parallel — same symptoms, different causes
- Named "the gap" — the disconnect between Amarvel self-reporting and ground reality
- Insight that unlocked Vaelen's anomaly discovery: "Deeper doesn't mean more distance. It means a different way of listening."

## The Ambition
- She intends to be in the rooms where decisions are made about the frontier
- The Solvarei rotation is a credential, not a destination
- She speaks FOR the frontier the way a lawyer speaks for a client
- She can respect someone and assess their utility simultaneously
- Pain that served no purpose is just pain — she stopped believing in purposeless suffering

## Key Relationships
- **Father:** Visits when deployments are close. Carries supplies. Talks to him. Counts three breaths with her hand on his shoulder.
- **Vaelen:** Respects his gift, frustrated he doesn't see its political value. First person to ask the right questions about her data. She shared her father's story — genuinely AND because she knows what that honesty does to a relationship.
- **Maret:** The neighbor who maintains what Drenna can't. Exchanges are minimal and loaded.

## Jungian Shadow (LOCKED — from Story DNA)
**GRIEF.** Not ambition — the ambition is surface. Drenna's true shadow is grief. The loss of her father, the loss of her community, the loss of the world she came from. The ambition is how she runs from it.

- Season 1: Credential-building → Partnership → Crisis accelerates timeline → Defining choice → Exit
- The Dread gives her the most powerful story a frontier politician could carry, and she knows it
- She grieves Caeleth AND she's already thinking about what this means. Both real.
- Father killed during the surge (Episode 4-5 timeframe)

## Voice (for POV chapters)
Short sentences. Physical, concrete. She names things, counts, files. Less interiority — she acts and the reader infers. Warmth buried under strategy. Humor dry and rare.

## RULES
- NEVER give her a political document, secret notebook, or physical prop for her ambition
- Every scene must show BOTH compassion AND ambition simultaneously
- She feels AND files. She grieves AND assesses. Neither cancels the other.
